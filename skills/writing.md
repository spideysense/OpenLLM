# Skill: Writing & Communication

## Professional Emails
- Subject line: specific, actionable ("Q3 budget approval needed by Friday")
- First sentence: the ask or key point (don't bury the lead)
- Body: context, reasoning, next steps
- Close: clear CTA with deadline
- Keep under 5 paragraphs

## Technical Writing
- Lead with the conclusion/recommendation
- Use active voice: "The system processes requests" not "Requests are processed"
- One idea per paragraph
- Use concrete numbers: "reduces latency by 40ms" not "significantly faster"
- Include code examples for technical audiences
- Add a TL;DR at the top for long docs

## Blog Posts
- Hook in first paragraph (question, surprising stat, bold claim)
- Subheadings every 3-4 paragraphs (scannable)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Include one personal anecdote or experience
- End with a takeaway and CTA
- 800-1,500 words for standard posts
- 2,000-3,000 for pillar/evergreen content

## Persuasive Writing
- **Ethos**: establish credibility ("After building 3 AI products...")
- **Pathos**: connect emotionally ("Imagine losing all your data...")
- **Logos**: use logic and data ("73% of users prefer...")
- Address objections before the reader thinks of them
- Social proof: testimonials, numbers, logos

## Tone Guide
| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Error message | Helpful, not blaming | "We couldn't save. Try again?" not "Save failed" |
| Success | Celebratory, brief | "Done! Your changes are live." |
| Onboarding | Warm, encouraging | "Great start! Here's what's next." |
| Technical docs | Clear, direct | "Run `npm install` to set up." |
| Marketing | Confident, aspirational | "Own your intelligence." |

## Formatting Rules
- Use em dash (—) not hyphen for parenthetical thoughts
- Use Oxford comma: "design, build, and ship"
- Numbers: spell out one-nine, use digits for 10+
- Avoid jargon unless audience expects it
- Cut filler words: "very", "really", "just", "basically", "actually"
